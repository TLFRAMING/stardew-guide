# Rogue Command Starter Choice Guide: Engineer, Economy, and Specialist

Rogue Command does not start with a single hero choice. Your opening is built from three parts: Engineer, Economy, and Specialist. New players often focus only on the Specialist, but the Engineer and economy choice are just as important.

## Engineer: Your Map Presence

The Engineer is the unit that lets you build, expand, and control the shape of the run. It is also a major failure point because losing it ends the run.

When choosing or playing around an Engineer, think less about damage and more about safety, positioning, and base tempo. A durable or forgiving Engineer is easier for beginners. More specialized options become stronger once you already understand map pressure and retreat paths.

## Economy: Your Production Ceiling

Your economy choice determines how reliably Crystal becomes buildings and units. A good Specialist cannot carry a run if you cannot afford production.

Judge economy options by reliability, risk, and protection needs. If your Harvester must travel far, secure the route. If your economy is efficient but fragile, keep combat units near the resource path. If your early income is slow, avoid spreading your build across too many production lines.

## Specialist: Your First Combat Identity

The Specialist gives the run its first tactical direction. Official and wiki-backed information points to several major identities: Castle, Incinerator, Robo Recycler, Support Tank, Swarm Walker, Mantis Mech, Robo Rush Beacon, and Phase Walker.

These should not be treated as a ranking chart. Castle leans toward defense and artillery pressure. Incinerator leans into fire and area pressure. Recycler uses losses as a resource. Support Tank protects key units. Swarm Walker creates infection-style pressure. Mantis Mech grows by consuming allied units. Rush Beacon and Phase Walker focus more on mobility, map presence, and disruption.

## A Beginner-Friendly Way to Choose

Do not start by asking which option wins every situation. Ask which complexity you want to practice. If you lose because your base falls apart, choose a steadier defensive route. If you enjoy map movement, try a mobility Specialist. If you want to learn build identity, pick one Specialist and play several runs in a row.

## The Rule of a Good Opening

A good opening does three things at the same time: keeps the Engineer safe, supports continuous production, and gives your army a clear early combat plan. If one of those is missing, your next rewards should repair it before you chase a complicated combo.
