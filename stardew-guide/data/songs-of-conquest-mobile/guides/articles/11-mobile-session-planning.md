# Songs of Conquest Mobile Session Guide: Plan Turns, Towns, and Battles on a Phone

Songs of Conquest Mobile works best when you treat each play session as a set of clean decisions, not a rush to clear every visible object on the map.

This guide helps phone and tablet players stop at better moments, resume more easily, and avoid losing track of campaign pressure.

## End At A Clean Decision Point

The worst time to stop is halfway through a confusing turn. When you return later, you may forget why a Wielder moved, what the town needed, or which fight was supposed to happen next.

Better stopping points:

- after finishing a battle
- after making a town purchase
- after choosing the next Wielder direction
- after collecting the resource that unlocks the next decision
- before entering a risky fight

If you have only a short session, aim to complete one clean loop: map check, town check, battle decision, stop.

## Use A Four-Part Turn Check

At the start of a mobile session, do not immediately move the nearest Wielder. First run this check:

1. **Objective:** what is the map asking for?
2. **Wielder:** who is carrying the main plan?
3. **Town:** what spending choice matters next?
4. **Battle:** which fight is worth taking now?

If one of those answers is missing, spend the session clarifying that answer instead of pushing forward blindly.

## Keep Notes In Your Head With One-Sentence Plans

You do not need a spreadsheet to play. You need a short sentence that explains the next few turns.

Useful examples:

- "Main Wielder clears the route, town saves for army support."
- "Collect the nearby resource, then decide the next purchase."
- "Avoid combat until the army can take the objective fight."
- "End after this battle and review the town next session."

This habit is especially helpful on mobile because interruptions are more common than on PC.

## Avoid The One-More-Turn Trap

One more turn is fine when the next action is safe and obvious. It becomes a problem when it leads into an unclear fight, an expensive town decision, or a new unexplored branch.

Stop instead if:

- the next fight needs careful positioning
- the town has several possible upgrades
- your main Wielder is exposed
- you are no longer sure what the objective requires
- you are tapping quickly just to keep moving

Stopping before a risky decision protects the next session.

## Resume With The Same Order Every Time

When you return to a save, use the same order:

1. Read the objective.
2. Find the main Wielder.
3. Check the town.
4. Check the army.
5. Decide the next fight or route.

This prevents the map from pulling you into random pickups before you remember the plan.

## Common Mobile Session Mistakes

- Ending during a half-finished turn.
- Starting a session by moving before checking the objective.
- Taking a battle because it is visible, not because it helps the plan.
- Spending town resources without checking what the Wielder needs.
- Leaving several Wielders with unclear jobs.
