# Haley Gifts Guide: Spring and Summer Friendship Planning

Haley friendship is easiest when you plan seasonal gifts before the season is almost over. Check the [Haley villager page](/stardew/villagers/haley), decide which gifts are safe to spend, and avoid using items that your farm economy or bundle plan still needs.

This guide is for players who want a simple Haley route without turning every flower, crop, or food item into a guess.

## Player Pain Point

Haley gift searches usually come from seasonal uncertainty. Players see flowers, forage, or crops and want to know whether they are good gifts. The risk is spending seasonal items too early and then needing them for another purpose.

## Why It Happens

Seasonal items feel abundant while they are appearing, then disappear quickly when the calendar changes. If you do not save a few copies, a gift choice can become a later shortage.

## Prevention Plan

Keep a small seasonal reserve before gifting heavily. If an item appears only in a season or comes from a crop plan, save a few copies first. After your reserve is safe, use extra items for Haley or other villagers.

Tie Haley gifting to town loops. If you already plan to visit shops, the beach, or other villagers, bring a planned gift. Do not make an entire day depend on finding one gift target.

## Recovery Plan

If you spent a seasonal item too early, stop using that item as a gift until the reserve is rebuilt. Switch to another known option or delay gifting until the next reliable source appears.

If the season is ending, prioritize items that cannot be replaced soon. Use the [forage database](/stardew/forage) and [crops database](/stardew/crops) to decide what should be saved.

## Practical Checklist

- Check Haley's gift list before spending seasonal items.
- Save a small reserve before gifting extras.
- Pair gifts with town, beach, or shop errands.
- Protect first-copy crops and forage until checked.
- Plan ahead before the final week of a season.

## Common Mistakes

- Giving away seasonal items without saving backups.
- Spending crop income on gifts too early.
- Waiting until the last day of a season to find gifts.
- Ignoring other villagers who may need the same item.
- Treating every liked gift as equally affordable.
