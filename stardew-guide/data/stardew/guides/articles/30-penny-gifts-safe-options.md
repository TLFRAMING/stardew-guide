# Penny Gifts Guide: Safe Friendship Choices for Early Farms

Penny is a good friendship target when you use planned gifts instead of spare items from your backpack. Check the [Penny villager page](/stardew/villagers/penny), protect bundle and cooking items, and gift from a small set of safe options.

The best early habit is simple: never give the only copy of an item until you know whether it has another use.

## Player Pain Point

Penny gift searches often happen because the player has a crop, forage item, or cooked dish and wants a yes-or-no answer. The danger is spending useful items during the same season when bundles, money, and recipes compete for storage.

## Why It Happens

Penny's friendship route overlaps with the same early-game item pressure as other villagers. A gift might be correct for friendship but wrong for your current farm if it blocks a bundle or removes an important saved item.

## Prevention Plan

Use a gift reserve. Keep a few known gift items separate from the sell bin and bundle chest. When you find a new item, check the [Community Center](/stardew/community-center) and villager page before gifting it.

Build Penny visits into town movement. If she is not on your route, save the gift for a better day rather than losing hours to a search.

## Recovery Plan

If you gave away a needed item, mark that item as protected in future storage. Replace it through normal farming, fishing, or foraging instead of changing the whole week around one mistake.

If Penny friendship lags, use planned weekly gifts and birthday timing to catch up.

## Practical Checklist

- Check Penny's current gift list before giving crops or dishes.
- Save first-copy items until bundle and recipe needs are clear.
- Keep gift items separate from sell-bin items.
- Combine Penny visits with normal town errands.
- Use [villager data](/stardew/villagers) when planning several friendship routes.

## Common Mistakes

- Giving the only copy of a seasonal item.
- Spending food or cooked dishes before checking ingredients.
- Making daily friendship trips that interrupt farm progress.
- Forgetting to protect bundle items.
- Treating birthdays as emergencies instead of preparing ahead.
